Case Studies

Real Examples
Case 01 · E-Commerce

Solo Etsy Seller Doubles Click-Through Rate

A maker selling handmade jewelry was spending weekends taking product photos with inconsistent lighting. She switched to an AI product photography tool — uploading a photo of the item against a plain background, and getting back a studio-quality image in under a minute. Her click-through rate on Etsy went from 2.1% to 4.4% in the first month.

💡 Takeaway: AI removes the cost barrier to looking professional.
Case 02 · Career

Job Seeker Lands Interview After 3 Months of Silence

A mid-career professional had been applying for 3 months with no callbacks. She pasted her resume and the job description into Claude and asked: "What's missing?" The AI identified that her resume used the wrong keywords for ATS systems, and her bullet points described duties rather than outcomes. She rewrote it in an afternoon. She got 2 interview calls within the first week.

💡 Takeaway: AI gives you a brilliant friend who will be brutally honest about your resume.
Case 03 · Small Business

Restaurant Owner Reclaims 5 Hours a Week

A local restaurant owner was spending hours each week responding to the same customer questions on Instagram DMs: "What are your hours?", "Do you do catering?", "Are you halal?" She set up a simple AI chatbot using a no-code tool. It now handles 80% of DM inquiries automatically, and she only steps in for complex requests. She now uses that saved time to work on a catering menu expansion.

💡 Takeaway: Small businesses can now afford tools that used to cost enterprise money.
Rik's Own Story

From PM to AI Builder — the 85/15 Principle

Rik built a full stock analysis platform with a DCF valuation engine — not by becoming a software engineer overnight, but by using AI to handle 85% of the code while applying his own domain expertise in the critical 15%: understanding which financial model was wrong and why. The same principle applies to anyone in this room. Your expertise is the moat. AI is the accelerant.

Speaker Notes

For Rik's Eyes
Opening hook

Don't start with slides. Open with: "Put your hand up if you've used AI in the last week." Then: "Keep it up if you used it for something work-related." Watch hands drop. That gap is exactly why we're here today.

Your credibility anchor

Mention RetailSnap AI and the stock analysis platform early — not to show off, but to establish that you've built things with AI, not just talked about it. "I'm not a researcher. I'm a product person who builds." That lands differently in a community room.

The "85/15" framing

This is your signature concept. Drive it home: AI does 85% of the work. But the 15% — knowing what good looks like, asking the right question, catching the hallucination — that's irreplaceable human expertise. You're not teaching them to be replaced. You're teaching them to use a superpower.

Handling the fear question

Someone will ask about jobs. Don't dismiss it. Acknowledge it: "It's a real and valid concern." Then reframe: "History shows technology eliminates tasks, not whole professions. The typist disappeared; the executive assistant evolved. The question is — do you want to be on the side that adapts, or the side that waits?" Then pivot to the career case study.

The live demo trick

Give the audience a task to do in parallel: "Take out your phone. Go to claude.ai. Type: 'I work in [their job]. What are 5 ways AI could save me time this week?' Don't overthink it. Just send it." While they type, you type the same thing on screen. Compare. It makes it real, not theoretical.

Closing line

"You don't need to become an engineer. You need to become someone who knows how to ask good questions. That's it. AI is the most powerful intern you've ever had — it just needs a good manager." Then hand out the one-page starter kit.
